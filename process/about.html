About Washington, DC

Brief history of DC

George Washington selected the site for Washington, DC, in 1791, combining land from Maryland and Virginia to establish the national capital.

The city was named after Washington, and the District of Columbia was named for Christopher Columbus.

The district is not a state, and has no voting representation in Congress. Congress established the local government that has been modified over time.
